Oral Cavity Overview

Divisions

  • Vestibule: Space between lips/cheeks and teeth
  • Oral cavity proper: Area behind teeth

Boundaries

  • Anterior: Lips
  • Posterior: Oropharynx
  • Superior: Hard/soft palate
  • Inferior: Tongue/floor of mouth
3

Tooth Classification

Primary Dentition

  • 20 teeth total
  • 8 incisors
  • 4 canines
  • 8 molars
  • Erupts 6-30 months

Permanent Dentition

  • 32 teeth total
  • 8 incisors
  • 4 canines
  • 8 premolars
  • 12 molars
  • Erupts 6-21 years
4

Tooth Morphology

Crown Components

  • Cusp: Elevated projection
  • Ridge: Linear elevation
  • Fossa: Depression
  • Groove: Shallow line
  • Pit: Point depression
5

Tooth Surfaces

Anterior Teeth

  • Labial: Lip side
  • Lingual: Tongue side
  • Mesial: Toward midline
  • Distal: Away from midline
  • Incisal: Biting edge

Posterior Teeth

  • Buccal: Cheek side
  • Lingual: Tongue side
  • Occlusal: Chewing surface
  • Mesial/Distal: As above
6

Tooth Numbering Systems

Universal System

Permanent: 1-32 (upper right to lower right)

Primary: A-T

Palmer Notation

Quadrant symbols with tooth numbers

FDI System

Two-digit system (quadrant + tooth)

7

Periodontal Structures

Components

  • Gingiva: Gum tissue
  • Periodontal ligament: Tooth suspension
  • Cementum: Tooth root covering
  • Alveolar bone: Tooth socket
8

Gingival Anatomy

Marginal Gingiva

Unattached collar around tooth

Attached Gingiva

Firmly bound to underlying bone

Interdental Papilla

Gingiva between teeth

Characteristics

  • Pale pink color
  • Stippled surface
  • Knife-edge margin
  • 1-3mm sulcus depth
  • Resistant to friction
9

Tongue Anatomy

Surfaces

  • Dorsum: Upper surface
  • Ventral: Undersurface
  • Lateral borders: Sides

Papillae Types

  • Filiform
  • Fungiform
  • Circumvallate
  • Foliate
10

Salivary Glands

Major Glands

  • Parotid: Largest, serous
  • Submandibular: Mixed secretion
  • Sublingual: Smallest, mucous

Functions

  • Lubrication
  • Digestion (amylase)
  • Antimicrobial
  • Buffering
11

Key Takeaways

  • Oral cavity contains complex anatomical structures
  • Teeth have specific morphology based on function
  • Periodontium supports teeth in alveolar bone
  • Salivary glands maintain oral environment
  • Understanding anatomy is crucial for clinical practice
